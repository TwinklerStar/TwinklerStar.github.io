About Me

I have just graduated as an Excellent Graduate with a bachelor’s degree from department of Electronics and Information Engineering, Beihang University in July, 2020. I was going to join the department of Electrical and Computer Engineering from University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign as a Ph.D. student this fall, but the unexpected Covid-19 messes up all my plans. Hopefully, our world can go back to normal so that I can continue my study.

Now, I am looking for an internship (a research assistant position is perfect) from 2020 fall to 2021 spring. If you are interested in working with me, please contact me through e-mail.

Research Experience

I’m broadly interested in the area of electromganetics, signal processing and human-computer interaction (HCI). From my perspective, basic research such as electromagnetics can make contributions to the development of HCI technique and the research in HCI can present a more clear view about underlying needs, application context and usability. So, combining these aspects together is a desirable way to create magical and delightful applications.

Since 2018, I have been an undergraduate researcher at the EMCTI under the supervision of Professor Qiang Ren. We focus on accelerating computational electromagnetics methods via deep learning.

During the summer of 2019, I was a visiting student researcher at the XDiscovery Lab from Dartmouth College, where I worked with Professor Xing-Dong Yang and Teyen Wu on smart cloth. From this experience, I developed a strong interest in the study of human-computer interaction. After that, I had a one week visit to the NDE Lab from Michigan State University, working with Professor Yiming Deng on the project Magna.

Previously, I had experience in signal processing in the project ADS-B and my first research project Multi-Antenna OFDM.
