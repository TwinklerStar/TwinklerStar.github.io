About Me

I have just graduated as an Excellent Graduate with a bachelor’s degree from Department of Electronics and Information Engineering, Beihang University in July, 2020. I was going to further my study by pursuing a Ph.D. degree in North America this fall, but the unexpected Covid-19 messes up all my plans. Hopefully, our world can go back to normal and I can continue my study.

Now, I am looking for an internship (a research assistant position is perfect) from 2020 fall to 2021 spring. If you are interested in working with me, please contact me through e-mail.

Research Experience

I’m broadly interested in the area of electromganetics, signal processing and human-computer interaction (HCI). From my perspective, basic research such as electromagnetics can make contributions to the development of HCI technique and the research in HCI can present a more clear view about underlying needs, application context and usability. So, combining these aspects together is a desirable way to create magical and delightful applications.

Since 2018, I have been working as an undergraduate researcher at the EMCTI under the supervision of Professor Qian Ren. We focus on accelerating computational electromagnetics methods via deep learning.

During the summer of 2019, I was a visiting student researcher at the XDiscovery Lab from Dartmouth College, where I worked with Professor Xing-Dong Yang and Te-yen Wu on smart cloth. From this experience, I developed a strong interest in the study of HCI. After that, I had a short visit to the NDE Lab from Michigan State University, working with Professor Yiming Deng on the project Magna.

Previously, I had experience in signal processing in the project ADS-B and my first research project Multi-Antenna OFDM.
